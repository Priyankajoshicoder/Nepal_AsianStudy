About Nepal

Quick Overview

Nepal, nestled between India and Tibet, is a country of diverse landscapes, from the towering Himalayas to subtropical forests. Home to over 30 million people, Nepal showcases an incredible mix of cultures, traditions, and natural wonders.

Capital: Kathmandu
Population: 30 million
Official Language: Nepali

History of Nepal

Before 800 BCE

Ancient Period

Early civilizations flourished in Nepal's fertile valleys. The Gopalas, Mahispalas, and Kirata dynasties ruled over different regions, establishing the foundations of Nepalese civilization..... Read More

300-800 CE

Licchavi Period

The Licchavi dynasty brought a golden age of art, architecture, and Sanskrit literature. Trade routes with Tibet and India flourished, bringing prosperity and cultural exchange..... Read More

1200-1768 CE

Medieval Period

The Malla dynasty ruled over the Kathmandu Valley, dividing it into three kingdoms: Kathmandu, Bhaktapur, and Patan. This period saw tremendous cultural and artistic development..... Read More

1768-2008

Modern Era

Prithvi Narayan Shah unified Nepal in 1768, establishing the Shah dynasty. This period saw Nepal's emergence as a modern nation-state, though facing various political challenges..... Read More

2008-Present

Contemporary Nepal

Nepal transitioned to a federal democratic republic, ending centuries of monarchy. The country now focuses on economic development, social progress, and preserving its rich cultural heritage..... Read More

Political Environment

Current Political System

Nepal is a federal parliamentary republic with a multi-party democracy. The country's current political system was established after the promulgation of the new constitution in 2015.

Government Type: Federal Parliamentary Republic
Constitution Adopted: September 20, 2015
Electoral System: Mixed Electoral System

Federal Structure

Nepal is divided into 7 provinces, with power shared between federal, provincial, and local governments.

  • 3 Tiers of Government
  • 7 Provinces
  • 753 Local Government Units
  • Independent Judiciary System
